What Is This Committee?

The Subcommittee on Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Protection is part of the US House of Representatives' Committee on Homeland Security. It is chaired by Chairman Fong, with Ranking Member Walkenshaw (D-VA) representing the minority.

The subcommittee examines national security threats linked to critical infrastructure, emerging technologies, and cyber vulnerabilities. It convenes expert hearings and produces legislative recommendations for the full Congress.

This hearing was called to examine — in the words of Chairman Fong — "a national security challenge that is currently unfolding inside the United States." The PRC is moving aggressively to dominate AI, robotics, and autonomous systems, already present "on American campuses, in American police departments, on American smartphones."

Official Purpose (Chairman Fong)
"To examine the national and economic security risks to the United States by artificial intelligence, robotics, and autonomous sensing technologies developed by companies affiliated with the People's Republic of China — and to evaluate whether existing federal guidance and supply chain security practices sufficiently address the risk."

08 · Policy Recommendations — to the Subcommittee
🛡 Restrictions & Bans
01
Extend ICTS framework to PRC robots — limiting importation unless China addresses security vulnerabilities. — Dr. Doshi
02
Prohibit federal agencies and contractors from procuring PRC robotic or AI systems. All four witnesses agreed. — All witnesses
03
Maintain semiconductor export controls, including inference chips inside robots. Limit smuggling pathways. — Dr. Doshi
04
Bans on PRC unmanned ground vehicles and companies on the 1260H list in sensitive environments. — Robbins
05
Direct CISA to evaluate foreign robots in critical infrastructure. Independent researchers found crucial vulnerabilities. — Malteno
🚀 Investment & Industry
01
Pass HR 7334 — National Robotics Commission Act. "Time is running out for policy efforts to have an impact." — Malteno
02
Treat US data as a national asset. Make federal data AI-ready. Investigate Chinese robotics data as national security threat. — Finkele
03
Mandate 5 flagship AI programs per agency by 2028. "One vs. 81. That is not a technology gap. It is a failure of will." — Finkele
04
Production tax credits, loan guarantees, advanced market commitments to scale domestic robot manufacturing. — Robbins
05
One national AI regulatory framework governing outputs, not inputs. "A patchwork of state rules is a competitive liability." — Finkele
🌐 Allies & Standards
01
Build allies-scale to rival China-scale. Preferential trading bloc: lower barriers among allies, raise them against China. "Collectively that bloc outscales China." — Dr. Doshi
02
Protect Japan's robotics input dominance. "If those companies disappear, the only game left is China." — Dr. Doshi
03
Align ICTS tool with allied partners to keep Chinese products out and give democratic nations time to rebuild. — Dr. Doshi
04
Export the American AI tech stack to allies before China does. Codify the executive order. "China won 5G because America didn't show up." — Finkele
05
Win AI standards via CAISI and allied safety institutes. "China began to win 5G when they won on standards." — Finkele
"We have every asset required to win. The talent, the capital, and the allies. The variable is urgency and the time to act is now."
— Max Finkele, Scale AI · Closing statement to subcommittee
