Technical architecture
Power flows from transmission grid down to each rack through four voltage transformation steps.
  • 🔌
    Power chain: 400 kV → 150 kV → 22 kV → 400 V AC
    Future megawatt racks will shift to medium-voltage DC at rack level to cut conversion losses.
  • 🧊
    Primary cooling: Atlantic seawater
    Air cooling viable to ~30 kW/rack. Current GPU racks: 130 kW. Future ~1 MW/rack requires direct liquid cooling.
  • 🔋
    Resilience: N+1 / N+2 redundancy
    UPS bridges 5–10 min. HVO generators with 24h on-site storage. Proven during April 2025 Iberian blackout — zero downtime.
  • 🌱
    Energy model: buy, don't build
    Long-term PPAs across solar, wind, hydro. Per GW consumed, equivalent new renewable capacity must be sponsored.
Buildout timeline — 6 buildings to 2030
Each building requires a 10+ year anchor tenant (hyperscaler or neo-cloud) to secure project finance. Microsoft announced a $10B investment post-recording. Staggered delivery reduces grid impact pressure.

Chapter 03 · The Mismatch

The Baseload Problem

The daily mismatch — a typical summer day
Solar and wind output swings wildly. Data centre load stays flat. The gap is filled by gas turbines or Spanish imports.
Seasonal clean energy coverage (estimated %)
Winter is wet and windy — Portugal's best season. Summer is the crunch: dry reservoirs, calm nights, peak cooling demand from the campus itself.
🔆 Best case: Jan–Apr
~90% clean
Wet season, full reservoirs, strong Atlantic winds. Gas rarely needed. Best months to claim near-100% renewable delivery.
☁️ Mid-season: Oct–Dec
~73% clean
Variable rainfall and wind. Hydro partially recharged. Still gas-dependent on calm nights.
☀️ Worst case: Jul–Sep
~60% clean
Dry reservoirs, solar daytime-only, calm nights. Up to 40% of campus hours backed by gas or imports.
Why PPAs don't fully solve this. Annual bookkeeping balances on paper. Physical hourly delivery does not. When the sun sets and the wind drops, the campus draws from Portugal's four gas CCGTs or Spanish imports — regardless of how many MW of solar PPA the operator holds. "100% renewable" is an accounting claim, not a physical grid reality at every hour of the year.
Chapter 04 · Grid Capacity Assessment

Can Portugal's Grid Handle It?

Three layers of analysis, three different answers — from manageable over the long run to a structural deficit at worst-case hours that cannot be solved without new firm generation.

The key arithmetic. Firm capacity at worst-case hours: gas 4.4 GW + partial hydro ~2.5 GW + biomass 0.7 GW = ~7.6 GW. Current national peak demand: 8.8 GW. Headroom before the campus: ~1.2 GW — exactly equal to its full draw. Any dry summer night at full load means Portugal must import from Spain or curtail other consumers.
✅
Green — manageable
Annual energy volume
PPAs + Portugal's 2030 renewable targets (20.8 GW solar, 12.6 GW wind) can match annual volume if build-out accelerates. Staggered campus delivery gives 6 years of runway.
⚠️
Amber — tight
Peak demand headroom
Portugal already at record import dependency (20% of 2024 supply). Adding 1.2 GW constant load (~14% of national peak) without new firm generation pushes imports structurally higher.
🔴
Red — critical gap
Firm night/summer power
Gas + constrained hydro + biomass ≈ 7.6 GW at worst hours vs. 8.8 GW existing peak. Campus adds 1.2 GW. Only gas expansion, long-duration storage, or SMRs bridge this — realistically mid-2030s.

Business & investment opportunities

The StartCampus build-out is the largest single demand signal the Portuguese energy market has ever seen. It creates a predictable, long-duration revenue anchor for sectors that have struggled to attract capital at scale.

🔆
Solar + storage hybrid parks
Now → 2028 · Highest certainty
Solar alone can't deliver night-time power — but solar + co-located BESS hybrid parks can. PNEC 2030 targets 20.8 GW solar (up from ~6 GW today). Every GWp paired with 4h storage is financeable against a campus PPA anchor.
PPA revenue · EPC · BESS supply chain
🌊
Floating offshore wind
2026–2032 · High strategic value
Portugal has 131 GW of offshore wind potential, 89% requiring floating foundations. Unlike solar, offshore wind generates at night. Framework approved April 2025. The campus sits directly below the Sines maritime zone in the spatial plan.
Floating foundations · Port logistics · O&M
🔋
Grid-scale battery storage (BESS)
2025–2027 · Active market now
Portugal's grid storage: ~13 MW vs a 2 GW 2030 target. Post-blackout BESS auctions target 750 MW by end 2025. Revenue stacking via capacity payments + day-ahead arbitrage + ancillary services creates a compelling investment case.
BESS development · Grid services · Ancillary market
💧
Green hydrogen hub at Sines
2027–2032 · Emerging
Portugal targets 5.5 GW of electrolysers by 2030. Electrolysers absorb surplus midday solar. Green H₂ stored and reconverted provides long-duration firm power for multi-day low-wind events. Sines port already handles LNG — natural hub candidate.
Electrolyser supply · H₂ storage · Port infrastructure
🏗️
Grid infrastructure & smart grid tech
2025–2030 · €3.5B committed
REN's grid cannot absorb 1.2 GW of new constant load without significant upgrade of transmission corridors between Sines and Lisbon/Porto. Post-blackout plan commits €3.5B to substation automation and power electronics.
EPC · Grid equipment · Smart substation
📊
24/7 CFE energy management platforms
2025–2028 · Software-first
Hyperscaler tenants have committed to 24/7 carbon-free energy matching by 2030. This requires real-time matching, certificate management, and demand-flexibility services. Portugal lacks this regulatory infrastructure — a greenfield software opportunity.
Energy software · VPP aggregation · CFE certificates
If Portugal gets this right: It becomes Europe's preferred AI infrastructure hub. The €50B campus investment catalyses a further €15–20B in grid and generation infrastructure, turning Sines into a new kind of industrial port city.
If Portugal gets this wrong: The campus draws gas power on every dry summer night. Import dependence on Spain deepens. Hyperscaler tenants miss their 24/7 CFE commitments. The opportunity migrates to Scotland or Norway.
